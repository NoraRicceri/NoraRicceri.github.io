What is a Choropleth map?

A choropleth map is a thematic map that makes use of different shades of colors to represent the value of specific variables across distinct regions. There are advantages and disadvantages to choropleth maps: they can be useful to represent specific set of data, such as population density or average income, they catch the readerâ€™s attention, and they can be relatively easy to read. On the other hand, they do not account for variation within the same country. It seems like the distribution inside a country is homogeneous.
